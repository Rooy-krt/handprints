Who Are We?

What Do We Do?

Handprints Society is a faith-driven, non-profit organization rooted in the heart of compassion. We exist to be the extended hands and feet of Christ reaching the forgotten, the vulnerable, and the overlooked. Our calling is to be a beacon of love in dark places, a vessel of hope for the broken, and a symbol of transformation for entire communities.

We serve not for recognition, but because we believe that every human being deserves the dignity of love, the power of opportunity, and the warmth of care. Inspired by the teachings of Jesus, we go where others hesitate, bringing education to children, healing to the sick, food to the hungry, shelter to the homeless, and the message of hope to the spiritually weary.

In the heart of Karia village, we have established New Vision School a growing beacon of hope for the children of West Pokot. This school, operating under Bushfire Ministry the umbrella organization for Handprints Society in Pokot started with just a handful of students learning under a tree and has now expanded to serve dozens of children, providing them with quality early childhood education, a feeding program, school supplies, and spiritual nurturing.

2. West Pokot Outreach Center

Karia village, West Pokot. Here, we responded to God's call to love those deemed unreachable. In a land where girls are married off at an early age and medical care is lacking, we planted a church and started a preschool which have become the seeds of transformation. We dream of building a full school and a dispensary to serve this community holistically.
